### Warnings:
- ⚠️ **`_save_scan_result()` is NOT called for all skip paths.** Developer stated "Always writes to logs/fed_scan_latest.json (even on skip)". Actual coverage:
  - **Saves:** `edge_below_threshold`, `confidence_below_threshold`, actionable signal ✅
  - **Does NOT save:** outside signal window, `fedwatch_unavailable`, `kalshi_markets_unavailable`, `no_classifiable_outcomes`
  - Impact: dashboard/monitoring may see a stale `fed_scan_latest.json` during these conditions. Not a trading safety issue — just a monitoring gap. Recommend fixing in next iteration by calling `_save_scan_result(...)` for all return paths in `get_fed_signal()`.

- ⚠️ **`run_fed_scan(dry_run)` param is unused.** `run_fed_scan()` accepts `dry_run: bool = True` but passes nothing to `get_fed_signal()`, which has no `dry_run` param. This is architecturally correct (execution is in `ruppert_cycle.py`), but the unused parameter is confusing. Suggest removing `dry_run` from `run_fed_scan()`'s signature or adding a `# dry_run: handled by caller` comment.

- ℹ️ **Redundant imports in step 4b.** `log_trade` and `log_activity` are imported at the top of `ruppert_cycle.py` (`from logger import log_trade, log_activity, ...`) but re-imported inside the `if DRY_RUN / else` blocks in step 4b. Harmless (Python caches imports), but messy. Suggest removing the in-block imports.
